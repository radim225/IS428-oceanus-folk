# VAST Challenge 2025 — Mini-Challenge 1: Full Task Details

## Background
Sailor Shift is Oceanus's biggest music superstar. She started in the
Oceanus Folk genre. In 2023 she joined the Ivy Echoes — an all-female
Oceanus Folk band (Sailor: vocalist, Maya Jensen: vocalist,
Lila "Lilly" Hartman: guitarist, Jade Thompson: drummer,
Sophie Ramirez: bassist). They broke up by 2026. Sailor's breakthrough
came in 2028 when a single went viral — the first Oceanus Folk song
ever to top global charts. She has released an album almost every year
since. She is passionate about (1) spreading Oceanus Folk, and
(2) helping lesser-known artists break into music.

Journalist Silas Reed is writing "Oceanus Folk: Then-and-Now" in 2040.
He has built a knowledge graph of artists, albums, songs, and influences.
Our task is to help Silas create visualisations to explore this data.

## Task 1 — Sailor Shift Career Profile (Charles)
Design and develop visualisations and visual analytic tools that will
allow Silas to explore and understand the profile of Sailor Shift's career.

Sub-questions:
- Who has she been most influenced by over time?
- Who has she collaborated with and directly or indirectly influenced?
- How has she influenced collaborators of the broader Oceanus Folk community?

Planned Tableau deliverables:
- Influence timeline (who influenced Sailor and when)
- Collaboration network graph (Sailor at centre, collaborators as nodes)
- Indirect influence tree (artists Sailor influenced, who then influenced others)
- Bandmate career tracker (Maya Jensen, Lila Hartman, Jade Thompson, Sophie Ramirez)

## Task 2 — Oceanus Folk Genre Spread (Klara)
Develop visualisations that illustrate how the influence of Oceanus Folk
has spread through the musical world.

Sub-questions:
- Was this influence intermittent or did it have a gradual rise?
- What genres and top artists have been most influenced by Oceanus Folk?
- How has Oceanus Folk changed with the rise of Sailor Shift? From which
  genres does it draw most of its contemporary inspiration?

Planned Tableau deliverables:
- Sankey/flow diagram (Oceanus Folk flowing into other genres over time)
- Genre influence timeline (line/area chart of rising impact 1993–2040)
- Top influenced artists bar chart
- Genre inspiration heatmap (genres feeding back into Oceanus Folk)

## Task 3 — Rising Star Prediction (Radim)
Use visualisations to develop a profile of what it means to be a
rising star in the music industry.

Sub-questions:
- Visualise the careers of three artists. Compare and contrast their
  rise in popularity and influence.
- Using this characterisation, give three predictions of who the next
  Oceanus Folk stars will be over the next five years.

Planned Tableau deliverables:
- Rising star radar/spider chart (defining breakout artist metrics)
- Career arc comparison (multi-line chart of 3 artists over time)
- Breakout candidate ranked list with visual evidence
- Prediction dashboard (candidates vs. rising star benchmark)

Top candidate artists from data: Orla Seabloom (8 notable works),
Beatrice Albright (8), Daniel O'Connell (8), Copper Canyon Ghosts (7),
Drowned Harbor (5)

## How the Tasks Connect
The three tasks form one narrative arc for Silas Reed's article:
- Task 1 (Charles) establishes Sailor Shift as the central case study
- Task 2 (Klara) shows how her genre spread outward to the broader music world
- Task 3 (Radim) uses patterns from Tasks 1 and 2 to predict who is next
Sailor Shift is the benchmark that anchors all three tasks.
Cross-dashboard Tableau filter actions should allow a user to click an
artist in Task 1 and see them highlighted in Tasks 2 and 3.
